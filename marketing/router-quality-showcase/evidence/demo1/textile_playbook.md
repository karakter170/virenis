# Approved Textile Playbook: Recycled-Offcut Tote Bags

Status: synthetic marketing-demo fixture
Purpose: provide a stable, auditable industry brief without making claims about current market statistics

## Material sourcing

- Textile offcuts vary by fiber content, color, thickness, and usable panel size, so incoming material should be sorted and labeled before production.
- Supplier agreements should define acceptable material types, contamination limits, collection frequency, and basic fiber-content records.
- Product claims should stay traceable to supplier records and the material actually used; avoid unsupported environmental claims.

## Product and quality

- Sample batches should be checked for shrinkage, color transfer, abrasion, and wash behavior before a material batch is approved.
- Tote-bag patterns should accommodate variable offcut dimensions while keeping the finished size and handle placement consistent.
- Load points, handle attachments, seams, and lining should receive explicit inspection because durability is central to the product promise.

## Operations and launch

- Begin with a small number of repeatable bag styles and limited material batches to control variation and simplify quality checks.
- A practical workflow is intake and sorting, batch testing, cutting, assembly, reinforcement, final inspection, labeling, and packing.
- Direct online sales, selected local retailers, and small corporate-gifting orders are suitable early channels for a small producer.
- The operating plan should account for irregular offcut supply, rejected batches, production rework, and the risk of inconsistent appearance between batches.
